# Art Provenance

Track production assets here as they are generated or imported. Every game asset should have a stable ID in `assets/asset_manifest.json`, and every non-placeholder production/imported asset should have a provenance key recorded here.

This project targets an MIT license, but generated assets still need clear provenance and cleanup records. Official logos are third-party/parody brand assets and are not original MIT-created artwork.

## Manifest Link Rules

- `assets/asset_manifest.json` is the ID registry and validation source.
- `provenanceKey` values in the manifest should map to a row or planned row in this document.
- `planned` manifest entries may point to future files that do not exist yet.
- `production`, `cleaned`, `raw`, and `concept` entries must have real source files and provenance notes before they land.
- `third_party_logo` entries must live under `assets/third_party/logos/`, include non-MIT ownership notes, and avoid any claim of endorsement.

## Intake Rules

- Official logos may be used for parody/faction presentation because the user explicitly wants them. Track each logo as a third-party/parody brand asset.
- Do not claim official logos are original project-created art.
- Do not mark official logos as MIT-included original artwork unless the asset license independently allows it.
- Keep official logos in `assets/third_party/logos/`.
- Record ChatGPT Images prompts for concept art.
- Record PixelLab prompts/settings for production pixel assets.
- Record manual cleanup done in Aseprite or Pixelorama.
- Mark assets as `planned`, `concept`, `raw`, or `cleaned` until they are game-ready.
- Production sprites must document dimensions, frame metadata, sprite origins, and isometric feet alignment.
